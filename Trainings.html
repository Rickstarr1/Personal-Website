The following instruments represent a curated selection of advanced technological systems that I have been formally trained to operate and have utilized extensively throughout my engineering career. Each tool has played a pivotal role in my hands-on research, enabling precise experimentation, data acquisition, and innovation in semiconductor development, materials characterization, and nanoscale fabrication. My proficiency with these instruments underscores my technical versatility and commitment to cutting-edge engineering methodologies.

SEM FEI NNL650

A high-resolution scanning electron microscope capable of sub-nanometer imaging. Features advanced detectors for material analysis and semiconductor defect inspection.

Trained at SNL & U of I

Temescal FCS2000

Advanced e-beam evaporation system with precise thickness control. Ideal for depositing uniform metal films in cleanroom environments.

Trained at SNL

VHX Keyence Digital Microscope

4K digital microscope with advanced depth composition technology. Enables real-time observation and measurement of complex surfaces.

Trained at SNL & U of I

Nanoscribe Quantum X

High-resolution 3D lithography system for microfabrication. Produces complex structures with 200 nm resolution for research and prototyping.

Trained at SNL

Leica EM ACE600

High-performance sputter coater with plasma cleaning capabilities. Ensures artifact-free sample preparation for electron microscopy.

Trained at SNL

Automated prober with micron-level positioning accuracy. Integrates with parameter analyzers for comprehensive device characterization.
