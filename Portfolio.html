Light-Activated Microstructures

During my time at Sandia National Laboratories, I worked with the Quantum X, a high-resolution two-photon 3D μ-printing system. Using this tool, I fabricated an array of hexagonal microstructures, each approximately 200 µm in diameter. To enhance their optical properties, I applied a reflective gold coating using electron-beam evaporation, ensuring anisotropic surface coverage for controlled reflectivity. Finally, I introduced a pH-sensitive hydrogel beneath the hexagonal structures. This hydrogel undergoes a physical transformation in response to pH changes, enabling dynamic modulation of the display—allowing the image to appear or disappear on demand.

Mesa Diode

I developed expertise in LED physics and engineering, focusing on carrier injection, radiative recombination dynamics, and critical efficiency metrics such as internal (IQE) and external quantum efficiency (EQE). I explored the design and fabrication of LEDs using III-V semiconductor materials (GaAs, InP, GaN), optimizing their performance for specific wavelengths across visible and infrared spectra. I have hands-on skills in characterizing LED behavior through I-V and L-I curve analysis, while addressing key challenges like efficiency droop and thermal management. By applying semiconductor theory to real-world optoelectronic systems, I gained the ability to evaluate and improve LED architectures for applications in solid-state lighting, displays, and optical communications.

OCT 2024

Smart Snack Dispenser

I led the development of the Smart Snack Dispenser, an automated portion-control system designed to promote mindful eating habits by precisely dispensing snacks in user-defined portions (15–70 g) including Skittles, M&Ms, and peanuts. This project completed all high-level requirements and system architecture, which integrates six specialized subsystems. Key components include a microcontroller (ESP32), touchscreen interface, stepper/DC motors, and a suite of four sensors for accurate dispensing and nutrition tracking. The team and I went through an iterative design process, verification testing results, and a full cost analysis. We achieved ±2 g dispensing accuracy and resolved any technical challenges we encountered throughout our process.

The project demonstrates my deep understanding of both digital design and retro gaming hardware architectures.
